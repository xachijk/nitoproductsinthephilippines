About Nito

Nito weaving, a traditional craft utilizing the durable and flexible Lygodium circinatum vine, thrives in various parts of the Philippines, particularly in Aklan and among the Iraya Mangyan of Mindoro. This vine, a secondary forest cover found in low to medium altitudes, grows abundantly in forested areas such as Altavas, Balete, Libacao, Madalag, and Buruanga in Aklan, and in the lands of the Iraya Mangyan. Its availability has made nito weaving an essential livelihood and cultural expression for these communities.

In Aklan, nito weaving has become a cornerstone of the local economy, producing items like placemats, bags, food trays, hats, and decorative jars that showcase the artistic skills of the Aklanons. The Department of Trade and Industry supports this industry by offering training programs to enhance skills and preserve the craft while providing sustainable livelihoods.

Similarly, the Iraya Mangyan are celebrated for their intricate nito weaving, creating unique and functional pieces such as baskets, trays, coasters, and jars. Their craftsmanship reflects individual creativity, as each weaver’s design is distinct. In Puerto Galera’s Talipanan Mangyan Village, tourists can experience their culture through guided tours and purchase their handcrafted products directly. Support from organizations like the Ayala Foundation has helped bring their creations to wider markets, promoting their heritage and providing new economic opportunities.

Together, these communities highlight the enduring cultural significance and economic potential of nito weaving, a tradition that connects artistry with sustainability and resilience.

Nito Plate

Nito Plates have a circular or oval shape and are crafted from woven Nito, featuring intricate patterns. They serve as a beautiful, eco-friendly alternative to traditional ceramic plates, adding a touch of natural elegance to dining settings.

 

 

  • Step-By-Step Procedure

    1. Preparing the Nito Vines: Collect mature Nito vines, usually found in forests.
    2. Starting the Base:
      • Take a few strips of Nito and bundle them into a tight coil to form the center.
      • Secure the coil by looping another strip of Nito around it tightly.
      • Gradually wrap and weave additional Nito strips outward to create a circular base.
    3. Weaving the Plate:
      • Continue weaving in a spiral motion, overlapping and tightening each strip to prevent gaps.
      • Adjust the height of the plate by layering strips along the edge, curving them upward if needed.
    4. Shaping and Smoothing:
      • Carefully cut off any protruding ends.
      • Tuck them into the weave for a seamless finish.
    5. Finishing Touches:
      • Allow the plate to air-dry completely.
      • Apply varnish or natural polish to give it a smooth finish and enhance durability.

    • Nito Placemat

    Nito Placemats are used to protect and decorate. They are rectangular and woven from Nito, featuring simple or easy patterns. These serve as a functional addition to dining settings, protecting table

     

     

     

  • Step-By-Step Procedure

    1. Preparing the Equipment: Clean and position the Nito placemats on a stable surface before starting to ensure a secure workspace.
    2. Preparing the Vines:
      • Soaking: Soak stiff vines in warm water to make them pliable.
      • Cleaning: Remove leaves, rinse, and strip unnecessary parts from the vines.
    3. Cutting and Dyeing:
      • Cut vines to uniform lengths (typically 30–40 inches).
      • Use natural dyes for coloring if desired, then let them dry completely.
    4. Weaving the Placemat:
      • Lay out several vertical strands as a base (warp).
      • Weave horizontal strands (weft) tightly over and under the warp strands.
      • Adjust the tension for an even weave.
    5. Securing the Ends:
      • Tuck ends into the weave or secure them with needle and thread.
      • Trim any frayed edges neatly.
    6. Finishing Touches:
      • Add a border for a polished look using thicker or colored vines.
      • Apply a thin layer of varnish or oil for durability and water resistance.
    7. Drying and Flattening:
      • Let the placemat dry completely if moist.
      • Flatten under a heavy object to maintain shape.

    The finished nito placemat features a rustic, natural look, offering a sturdy yet flexible design with a visually appealing woven texture. Its organic color and texture make it a perfect choice for dining or decorative settings.

    • Nito Basket

    Nito baskets are handmade from nito palm fibers, known for their strength and flexibility. These baskets are both functional and decorative, offering a natural, rustic charm.

     

     

     

  • Step-By-Step Process

    1. Gathering and Preparing the Nito
      • Harvesting - Nito fibers are carefully harvested from the leaves of the nito palm to ensure the plant remains undamaged.
      • Soaking - The fibers are soaked in water for several hours to make them softer and easier to weave.
      • Drying - After soaking, the fibers are dried to remove moisture and prevent rotting.
    2. Weaving the Basket
      • Creating the Base - A circular base is made by weaving sturdy spokes with thinner, flexible nito fibers.
      • Building the Sides - The sides are woven upward from the base, shaping the basket as desired.
      • Adding Handles - Handles are woven from multiple strands of nito and attached to the basket for added functionality.
    3. Finishing Touches
      • Trimming - Excess fibers are trimmed for a smooth, polished look.
      • Polishing - The basket is polished with natural oil or wax for added shine and durability.

    The finished nito basket is a sturdy, versatile item with a natural, handcrafted appeal, perfect for everyday use or as a decorative piece.

    • Nito Tray

    A Nito Tray is a handmade tray made from Nito vines, a type of plant found in the Philippines. The vines are dried and woven into sturdy, beautiful designs. These trays are used for serving food, organizing items, or as decoration, adding a natural and stylish touch.

     

     

  • Step-By-Step Procedure

    1. Prepare the Nito Vines
      • Clean the nito vines to remove dirt and debris.
      • Soak the vines in water for 15–20 minutes to make them more flexible and easier to weave.
      • Dry the vines slightly with a cloth to remove excess water.
    2. Start the Weaving Process
      • Weave the nito in a desired pattern (e.g., over-under, spiral, or crisscross).
      • Maintain even tension throughout the process to ensure a neat and uniform finish.
    3. Build Up the Tray Sides
      • Once the base is complete, continue weaving upward to form the tray's sides.
      • Follow a consistent weaving pattern to achieve uniformity.
    4. Secure Loose Ends
      • Tuck in or tie off loose vine ends securely to prevent unraveling.
    5. Reinforce the Structure
      • Wrap thin rattan strips around weak areas or joints to strengthen the tray.
      • Ensure all reinforcements are tightly wrapped and secure.
    6. Trim
      • Trim excess vines using scissors or cutters to achieve a clean, polished edge.
    7. Apply Finishing Touches
      • If desired, apply a layer of varnish or lacquer to enhance the nito’s natural sheen and provide protection.
      • Allow the tray to dry completely before use.

    The final product is a durable, versatile tray with a beautiful, rustic aesthetic that showcases the natural beauty of the nito vines. Whether used for serving or as a decorative piece, it adds charm and character to any setting.

    • Nito Rings

    Nito rings for blind curtains are decorative, functional rings made from flexible natural Nito vine. These rings are used for holding curtain rods and are popular for their rustic and organic look. They provide a unique, eco-friendly touch to window treatments, offering durability and ease of use.

     

     

  • Step-By-Step Procedure

    1. Preparation of Materials
      • Select nito vines that are flexible but strong enough to hold their shape once formed.
      • Cut the vines to the desired size, depending on the dimensions of the ring you want to make.
    2. Form a Ring Shape
      • Take one piece of nito and bend it into a circular shape to form a complete ring.
      • Trim any excess length with scissors, leaving just enough to slightly overlap the ends.
    3. Secure the Ends of the Ring
      • Overlap the ends slightly where the ring closes.
      • Use a hot glue gun to hold the ends together or wrap a piece of wire tightly around the overlap for extra stability.
    4. Reinforce the Ring
      • For added rigidity, wrap more wire around the base of the ring. This ensures the ring retains its shape and strength.
      • If a natural look is preferred, omit the wire reinforcement and rely solely on glue for a seamless appearance.
    5. Finishing the Ring
      • Allow the glue to dry completely or ensure the wire is secure.
      • For a polished appearance, apply stain or varnish to the Nito. Let the finish dry thoroughly before proceeding.
      • Add decorative elements, such as colored thread, fabric, or small beads, for a personalized touch.
    6. Repeat the Process
      • Create additional rings following the same steps. The number of rings required depends on your curtain or blind design and the number of tiebacks needed.
    7. Attach the Rings to the Curtain
      • Pass a cord or metal hook through each Nito ring and attach them to the curtain header.
      • Alternatively, sew small loops or hooks onto the curtain fabric and slide the rings through for a functional yet decorative touch.
    8. Install the Curtain Tiebacks
      • Use the Nito rings as part of the curtain tieback design, either by hanging the tiebacks over the rings or draping the curtains directly over the rings for support.

    The completed Nito Rings bring a charming, natural aesthetic to any space while remaining practical for use in curtain and blind installations. Customize them to suit your personal style and enjoy the added elegance they bring to your home.
